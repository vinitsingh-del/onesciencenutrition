# Controlled Impact

The banner should feel premium through containment, not chaos. Motion and athlete energy are allowed, but the composition must protect the sales message with deliberate safe zones, clear margins, and a stable hierarchy. The product is the purchase object, the ambassador is the trust object, and the black-gold system is the brand object.

The visual language uses disciplined contrast: near-black fields, controlled gold accents, clean product staging, and a bounded athlete image that never floods the interface. Every element should look placed, not spilled. This is CRO design with editorial restraint.

The banner must have master-level execution: no text should be embedded where the live page already carries copy, no focal point should be cropped awkwardly, and no image should depend on `cover` cropping to look correct. It should feel crafted for the exact viewport, with separate desktop and mobile compositions rather than one asset stretched into two jobs.
